Welcome to my Intro to Information Technology lecture website. Please browse the website. Any constructive feedback or commentary is greatly appreciated!
What is your interest in IT?
I have had long interest in IT. I started working as an application and database developer in the late 90's. I left the industry in the early 2000's to start the first of several of my own businesses. Mostly, I find the problem solving and the ability to make your own tools very satisfying. Networks are interesting and a recent Rasberry Pi purchase has inspired me to start building a basic computer from scratch using breadboards and EEPROM chips.
When did your interest in IT start?
I began to get interested in computers in the 80's with an Apple 2e computer. It was mostly playing games, but eventually, led to some basic programming.
Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest in IT?
I always had a background interest in computers, so when things really started to explode in the 90's with the growing popularity of the web, I decided to focus on on programming and database developement.
Outline your IT experience
I've been out of the industry for a long time. I worked as a programmer and database developer in the 90's and early 2000's. I jumped ship to start a business in the food industry. It was always useful to know how to set up a server and automate administrative processes. More recently, I have been involved in CNC machines and CAD and CAM programming. I recently completed a Linux administration certification which led me to want to pursue the Bachelor of IT with RMIT.
Why did you choose to come to RMIT?
It appeared as though RMIT has been focused on offering web based courses for awhile. The university has good reputation and the courses on offer seemed to fit what I was looking for.
What do you expect to learn during your studies?
I'm looking to get a broad range of skills. In particular, I hoping to update my knowledge in programming and to also learn about more modern project methodologies and design principles.
